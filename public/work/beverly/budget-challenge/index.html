Beverly, Massachusetts · Try it yourself

"The money is there. They just need to find it."

It's the most common thing people say about the city budget, and it's a fair thing to wonder. So here's the city's actual budget, with a chance to try your hand at it. In fiscal year 2026, Beverly came up about $3.9 million shortsource. See if you can close the gap, and get a feel for what the choice really involves.

A few ground rules, and they're the real ones the city lives with:
  • Some of the budget is locked. The city legally can't touch it this year. Those boxes explain why, and when it changes.
  • The rest is services, and almost every dollar is somebody's job. You can cut them, but the cut has consequences.
  • It has to be permanent money. One-time tricks don't count, because the shortfall comes back every year.

How this works. Every department total is real, taken from the City of Beverly's FY2026 budget. Because most city spending is salaries, the job estimates assume an average position, with benefits, costs about $95,000; real pay varies, so treat the counts as ballpark. This is a simplified model at the department level, not a line-by-line ledger, and the consequences are reasoned estimates, not predictions.

What the city actually did. It closed this year's gap with a mix: a higher trash fee, real cuts across departments, and the last of its one-time funds.source And the gap grows in the years ahead (toward $6.3M, then $7.6M, then $10.2M if nothing structural changes), so balancing it once doesn't make it go away.
